What is a CDN?

A Content Delivery Network is a network of servers in many different locations that helps deliver web content such as images and videos more quickly and reliably. By storing copies of this content closer to the users also known as caching, CDNs reduce loading times and improve the overall experience especially during busy times, the CDNs help websites run faster and stay accessible from different locations.


Different types of CDNs:

  • Traditional CDNs: Focus on static content delivery like loudflare.
  • Dynamic CDNs: Optimises the delivery of dynamic content such as Amazon CloudFront.
  • Private CDNs: Operated by organisations for internal use only, often to protect and secure data.
  • Gaming CDNs: For online gaming, providing low latency and higher performance
